ALL TRADITIONAL STREET BEVERAGE
비빔밥
(Bibimbap)

rice topped with vegetables, meat and optional red pepper paste, served in hot stone pot.
팥빙수
(Patbingsu)

It's red bean paste, milk, ice cream, rice cake and fruits on ice flake.
붕어빵
(Bung-eo-bbang)

Fish shaped pastry that is filled with red bean paste. Also very popular in the winter
불고기
(Bulgogi)

Bulgogi is prepared by marinating thin slices of beef and grilling them.
치킨
Chicken

like chicken wings in the United States, they are downed with beer or soju, after work or after dinner, rarely eaten as a meal.
김밥
(Gimbap)

Strips of ham, pickled radish, seasoned spinach, and egg are then placed close together on the rice. it is then carefully rolled together until the roll is evenly shaped.
김치
(Kimchi)

made of cabbage, garlic and Korean spices. Koreans eat it with rice and soup.
삼겹살
(Samgyeopsal)

Samgyeopsal is grilled pork. Korean place a piece of Samgyeopsal on a lettuce or a sesame leaf, then wrap it and eat as a whole.
소주
(Soju)

Comparable to vodka but less potent, soju is the most popular traditional Korean liquor among the general public. Soju was originally brewed from grains
떡볶이
(ddeok-bokk-i)

Maybe the most popular street food in Korea. Rice cakes are dipped in spicy sauce and eaten. Very popular in the winter.
